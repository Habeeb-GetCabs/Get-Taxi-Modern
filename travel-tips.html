5 Essential Tips for Safe Hill Driving in the Ghats

Driving through the Western Ghats to destinations like Ooty, Munnar, or Valparai offers some of the most breathtaking scenery in India. However, navigating the hairpin bends and steep gradients requires a specific set of skills. At Get Taxi Kovai, our company ensures all the fleet drivers in our network are highly trained for these conditions. Whether you are driving yourself or riding with us, here are five essential pro tips for safe mountain driving.

1 Master Engine Braking (Lower Gears)

When descending a steep hill, do not rely solely on your foot brake, and never coast in neutral. Constantly riding the brakes will cause them to overheat and eventually fail. Instead, shift into a lower gear (like 2nd or 3rd) and let the engine naturally slow the vehicle down. The golden rule is to use the same gear going downhill that you would use to climb that exact same hill.

2 Uphill Traffic Has the Right of Way

If you are driving downhill and the road narrows, you must yield to the vehicle climbing up. Vehicles going uphill require more momentum and effort from the engine; forcing them to stop and restart on a steep incline can be dangerous or cause stalling. Always pull over to a safe spot and let the climbing traffic pass.

3 Honk on Blind Curves and Hairpin Bends

The Ghats are filled with blind corners. Always use your horn when approaching a curve to alert oncoming traffic. If you are driving in low light or fog, flash your headlights before making the turn. Heavy vehicles like buses will often swing wide into the opposite lane to make tight hairpin turns, so stop well in advance and give them room to maneuver.

4 Never Overtake on Curves

Patience is vital. Never overtake another vehicle on a hairpin bend or a blind slope. You simply cannot see what is coming from the other side. Only pass slower vehicles when you have a completely clear line of sight on a straight stretch of road. Remember, mountain driving is not a race; arriving safely is the only goal.

5 Prepare Your Vehicle

Before attempting the Ghats, ensure your vehicle is in peak condition. Balding tires will not provide enough grip on steep, wet roads. Check your tire tread depth, ensure proper tire pressure, and top off your coolant and brake fluids before your ascent. Due to patchy mobile networks in the hills, it is also highly recommended to download offline maps before starting your trip.
