Munnar vs Kodaikanal: Which to Choose?

When our clients at Get Taxi Kovai book a weekend escape from Coimbatore, the hardest choice is almost always between the Kashmir of South India (Munnar) and the Princess of Hill Stations (Kodaikanal). Both are roughly 4.5 hours away, but they offer completely different experiences. Here is our ultimate guide to help you decide.

The Case for Munnar (Kerala)

Munnar is the undisputed king of tea estates. If you are looking for endless rolling green carpets, misty mountain peaks, and romantic weather, Munnar is the winner. The journey from Coimbatore (approx. 155 KM) is an absolute thrill.

The Drive: Udumalpet & Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary

To reach Munnar, your Get Taxi Kovai cab will pass through Udumalpet and cross the Kerala border right into the Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary. This is a protected forest reserve.

  • Wildlife Sightings: It is incredibly common to spot wild elephants, deer, and peacocks right from your cab window as we drive through the forest.
  • Marayoor Sandalwood: You will also pass through Marayoor, the only place in Kerala with natural sandalwood forests. Roll down the windows and smell the air!

The Highlights of Munnar

Once you arrive, Munnar is all about nature. Eravikulam National Park is a must-visit to see the endangered Nilgiri Tahr. Mattupetty Dam offers speed boating, and Echo Point is famous for its natural acoustic resonance.

Best for: Honeymooners, nature photographers, and travelers who want to escape the city noise and get lost in the greenery.

The Case for Kodaikanal (Tamil Nadu)

If Munnar is wild and green, Kodaikanal is structured, pedestrian-friendly, and vibrant. Centered entirely around a massive star-shaped lake, Kodai feels like a classic colonial summer retreat.

The Drive: Palani Hills Ghat Road

The route from Coimbatore to Kodai (approx. 170 KM) takes you through the temple town of Palani. The Palani Ghat road is famous for its steady incline and majestic views of the plains below. The roads are slightly wider than the Munnar route, making it a very comfortable ascent in our Premium SUVs.

Kodai Lake & Coaker's Walk

Kodaikanal Lake is the heart of the town. You can rent a bicycle and ride the 5 KM path around the water, go horseback riding, or take a rowboat out. Just a short walk away is Coaker's Walk, a 1-kilometer paved pathway running along the edge of steep slopes, offering stunning views of the valley.

Pillar Rocks & Pine Forests

Kodaikanal is famous for its dense, mystical Pine Forests—a favorite shooting spot for South Indian movies. The Pillar Rocks are three giant rock formations standing 400 feet tall, piercing through the clouds.

Best for: Families with kids, friend groups, and travelers who want activities (cycling, boating, cafes) right outside their hotel.
