The Ultimate Coimbatore to Ooty & Coonoor Travel Guide

The journey from Coimbatore to the Nilgiris is a traveler's dream. With lush green landscapes, thrilling hairpin bends, and iconic heritage spots, every kilometer offers something new. At Get Taxi Kovai, this is our most popular route. Here is an in-depth guide covering the journey and the magnificent spots across Coonoor and Ooty to help you plan your ultimate hill-station escape.

The Journey Begins: Coimbatore to Mettupalayam

Your ascent to the Queen of Hill Stations starts by driving from Coimbatore towards the foothill town of Mettupalayam. The route is incredibly scenic, passing close to the beautiful Bhavani River. Mettupalayam serves as the gateway to the Nilgiris, where the plains meet the massive mountains.

Must-Visit En Route: Black Thunder Theme Park

If you want to add some thrill before climbing the hills (or dedicate a full day to it), Black Thunder Water Theme Park in Mettupalayam is an excellent choice. Set against the stunning backdrop of the Nilgiris, this massive water park is a favorite for families.

  • Timings: The park is open daily from 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
  • Entry Fee: Adult tickets are priced around Rs. 990 to Rs. 1,090. For children (between 85 cm and 140 cm in height), tickets cost around Rs. 840 to Rs. 850.
  • Student Discounts: School students can enter for Rs. 790, and college students for Rs. 890 (a valid ID or bonafide letter is required).

The Climb: 36 Hairpin Bends

After crossing Mettupalayam, the real adventure begins. The ghat road taking you up to Coonoor and Ooty features exactly 36 hairpin bends. Each bend elevates you further into the misty, cool climate of the Western Ghats, taking you through dense forests and aromatic spice plantations. Our expert mountain-certified drivers at Get Taxi Kovai navigate these sharp curves smoothly, ensuring a completely nausea-free, comfortable ride for you and your family.

Exploring Coonoor: The Hidden Gem

Often considered Ooty's quieter, more serene sister, Coonoor sits at a slightly lower altitude. It is famous worldwide for its sprawling tea estates, dramatic cliffside viewpoints, and colonial heritage. Here are the absolute must-visit spots in Coonoor:

1. Sim's Park

Established over a century ago in 1874 by Major Murray and J.D. Sims, this meticulously maintained botanical garden spans 12 hectares. It is a nature enthusiast's paradise, home to over 1000 species of colorful flowers and rare, exotic trees from around the globe. Visitors can explore meandering walkways lined with lush flora, a dedicated rose garden, a glasshouse, and even enjoy paddle boating in the park's small lily pond.

  • Timings: Open daily from 7:00 AM to 6:30 PM.
  • Entry Fee: Rs. 75 per adult and Rs. 40 per child (3-12 years).

2. Dolphin's Nose

Located about 12 km from Coonoor town, this spectacular natural viewpoint sits at an altitude of approximately 1,550 metres above sea level. The rocky protrusion bears an uncanny resemblance to the nose of a dolphin jutting out dramatically from the hillside. Standing at the fenced edge, you are rewarded with breathtaking panoramic views of rolling green tea estates, deep ravines, and the magnificent Catherine Falls sparkling in the distance.

  • Timings: Open every day from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
  • Entry Fee: Budget-friendly, ranging from Rs. 15 to Rs. 40 per person depending on the season, with a separate parking fee.

3. Lamb's Rock

Situated in Burliar village about 8 km from Coonoor, Lamb's Rock is another phenomenal cliffside viewpoint. Named after Captain Lamb, who originally developed the site out of personal interest, the cliff stands an impressive 5000 feet above sea level. It offers uninterrupted, sweeping views of the lush Hulical ravines and the distant Coimbatore plains. The drive to Lamb's Rock takes you through dense, aromatic tea gardens, making the journey just as memorable as the destination.

Discovering Ooty: The Essential Stops

After exploring Coonoor, continue your ascent to the heart of the Nilgiris—Ooty. Known for its rich colonial heritage and vibrant landscapes, Ooty has several iconic spots that define the ultimate hill station experience:

Government Botanical Garden

Spread over 55 acres, this historic garden is the vibrant heart of Ooty. It features stunning terraced layouts, a mesmerizing collection of exotic ferns, an Italian-style garden, and a fossilized tree trunk believed to be over 20 million years old. It is a fantastic spot for family picnics and nature photography.

Ooty Lake & Boat House

Constructed in the 1820s, this massive artificial lake is surrounded by tall, fragrant eucalyptus trees. Hiring a rowboat, pedal boat, or motorboat and gliding through the misty waters is one of the most quintessential Ooty experiences you cannot miss.

Doddabetta Peak

Standing at over 2,600 metres, Doddabetta is the highest peak in the entire Nilgiri mountain range. The panoramic view from the summit stretches across the entire valley, and on a clear, cloudless day, you can even spot the plains of Coimbatore and the distant highlands of Mysore!
