Exploring Valparai: The 40 Hairpin Ride

If popular hill stations like Ooty and Kodaikanal feel a bit too crowded for your taste, it is time to turn your attention to Valparai. Located roughly 105 kilometers from Coimbatore, this untouched paradise sits high in the Anamalai mountain range. It offers pristine tea estates, dense forests, and a much quieter, nature-focused escape. But getting there is half the adventure.

Navigating the 40 Hairpin Bends

The Pollachi - Aliyar Route

The journey begins with a smooth drive towards Pollachi, followed by the scenic Aliyar Dam. Just past the monkey falls checkpoint, the real climb begins. The route to Valparai features exactly 40 hairpin bends.

  • Loam's View Point (9th Hairpin Bend): Ask your driver to pull over here. This spot offers a breathtaking, bird's-eye view of the Aliyar Dam reservoir spread out below.
  • Wildlife Sightings: As you drive through the Anamalai Tiger Reserve, keep your cameras ready. It is common to spot the endangered Lion-tailed macaques, Nilgiri Tahr, and Malabar giant squirrels crossing the road.

Top Things to Do in Valparai

Unlike commercialized hill stations, Valparai is a protected area. There are fewer large hotels and noisy tourist traps, making it perfect for peaceful exploration.

Sholayar Dam

The second deepest dam in Asia. The drive from Valparai town to Sholayar is incredibly scenic, surrounded by lush green tea carpets and dense canopy forests.

Koolangal River

A shallow, crystal-clear river flowing right through a tea estate. It is completely safe for a refreshing dip, making it a favorite spot for families.

Nallamudi Viewpoint

Requires a short 1 KM walk through a tea estate, but rewards you with majestic views of the Anamudi peak and spectacular waterfalls plunging into the valley.

Athirapally Extension

If you have an extra day, you can continue past Sholayar Dam into Kerala to visit the massive Athirapally Waterfalls, often called the Niagara of India.
